Pollution

The Problem

In the widely industrialized world we live in, pollution has become a large problem. The largest and most well-known effect of pollution is global warming. Other effects of pollution include:

  • Environment Degradation
  • Respiratory Problems
  • Ozone Depletion
  • Infertile Land

The Causes

There are various causes of pollution in the world around us. Some of these causes include:

  • Burning of fossil fuels - Sulfur dioxide emitted from the combustion of fossil fuels like coal, petroleum, and other factory combustibles is one of the major causes of air pollution.
  • Agricultural activities - Ammonia is a very common by product from agriculture related activities and is one of the most hazardous gases in the atmosphere.
  • Indoor air pollution - Household cleaning products and painting supplies emit toxic chemicals in the air and cause air pollution.

The Solution

So what can you do to help decrease air pollution?

  • Use public transportation - Encourage people to use public transportation to reduce pollution.
  • Conserve energy - Switch off fans and lights when they are not necessary.
  • Reduce, reuse, recycle - Do not throw away items that are not useful for you. Reuse them for another purpose, or recycle them if the option is available.

Air pollution is the contamination of air by smoke and harmful gases, mainly oxides of carbon, sulfur, and nitrogen.

Land pollution is the degradation of the Earth's surface caused by a misuse of resources and improper disposal of waste.

Light pollution is the brightening of the night sky inhibiting the visibility of stars and planets by the use of improper lighting.

Noise pollution is any loud sounds that are either harmful or annoying to humans and animals.

Thermal pollution is the increase of temperature in an area caused by human activity.

Visual pollution is what you would call anything unattractive or visually damaging to the nearby landscape.

Water pollution is the contamination of any body of water (lakes, groundwater, oceans, etc).

Recycling is the process of collecting and processing materials that would otherwise be thrown away as trash and repurposing them.

Energy conservation refers to reducing energy consumption through using less of an energy service.
